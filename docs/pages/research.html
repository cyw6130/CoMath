Research

Exploring the topology of low-dimensional spaces

Overview

My research focuses on the beautiful interplay between algebra and topology, particularly in dimensions 3 and 4. I am fascinated by how abstract algebraic structures — tensor categories, quantum groups, and representation theory — give rise to powerful invariants that can distinguish between different topological spaces.

Current Focus: Non-semisimple TQFTs and their applications to 3-manifold invariants.

Research Areas

Quantum Topology

Quantum topology studies topological invariants arising from quantum groups and related algebraic structures. These invariants often have deep connections to:

  • Mathematical Physics: Chern-Simons theory, conformal field theory
  • Representation Theory: Quantum groups at roots of unity
  • Category Theory: Tensor categories, braided monoidal categories

Turaev-Viro Invariants

The Turaev-Viro construction produces 3-manifold invariants from spherical fusion categories. Given a triangulation \mathcal{T} of a 3-manifold M:

TV(M) = \sum_{\text{colorings}} \prod_{\text{tetrahedra}} |6j|^2

These invariants are:

  • Topologically invariant (independent of triangulation)
  • Computable (via state-sum formulas)
  • Related to Reshetikhin-Turaev invariants via the Turaev-Walker theorem

Non-Semisimple Extensions

When working with non-semisimple categories, new phenomena emerge:

  • Modified Traces: The usual categorical trace fails; we need renormalized traces
  • Logarithmic CFT: Physical interpretation involves non-unitary theories
  • New Invariants: Potentially stronger invariants than the semisimple case

This is an active area with many open questions!

Knot Theory & Categorification

Khovanov homology categorifies the Jones polynomial V_L(q) of a link L:

V_L(q) = \sum_{i,j} (-1)^i q^j \dim \text{Kh}^{i,j}(L)

The categorified invariant \text{Kh}(L) is a bigraded chain complex that:

  • Detects the unknot (Kronheimer-Mrowka, 2011)
  • Provides bounds on slice genus
  • Has connections to symplectic geometry and gauge theory
Note

Open Problem: Does Khovanov homology detect the trefoil knot?

Geometric Topology

Heegaard Splittings

Every closed, orientable 3-manifold M admits a Heegaard splitting: M = H_g \cup_\phi H_g where H_g is a genus-g handlebody and \phi is a gluing homeomorphism of the boundary.

Key Questions:

  • How does the Heegaard genus relate to other complexity measures?
  • What is the relationship between Heegaard diagrams and quantum invariants?

Triangulations & Pachner Moves

The Pachner moves provide a finite set of local modifications that connect any two triangulations of a manifold:

Dimension Moves
2D 2-2 flip
3D 1-4, 2-3, and their inverses
4D 1-5, 2-4, 3-3, and their inverses

These moves are fundamental for:

  • Proving topological invariance of state-sum models
  • Computational topology algorithms
  • Understanding the geometry of the space of triangulations
